INNOVATIVE SOUNDS

Discover new music while enjoying the sounds of classic prog.

MARCH 2025

This Women's History Month, join us as we spotlight the innovators whose unique voices and bold creativity continue to shape the genre.

A Natural Disaster Anathema

Lee Douglas's vocal on A Natural Disaster marked a turning point for Anathema, introducing a new emotional depth to the band's evolving sound. Originally rooted in doom metal Anathema shifted towards progressive rock in the 2000s, and Lee's voice became a vital part of that transformation.

Her performance on this track is both fragile and powerful, and captures the song's sense of longing and introspection.

No Longer We Lie Exploring Birdsong

Liverpool-based trio Exploring Birdsong captivates listeners by seamlessly merging progressive rock, pop, and classical influences and vocalist/keyboardist Lynsey Ward is central to the band's distinctive style.

No Longer We Lie, the closing track from their 2023 EP Dancing in the Face of Danger showcases the band's innovative approach to progressive music.

Becoming, (R)evolution Phase Transition

Hailing from Porto, Portugal, Phase Transition, blends classical and technical progressive metal. At the center is Sofia Beco, whose violin and vocals shape the band's distinct sound.

According to Sofia, Becoming, (R)evolution, from their upcoming album In Search of Being, is about 'youth, growing up — that time of your life when you learn you have a voice.'

Holy Ghost Bent Knee

Known for blending pop, industrial rock, progressive rock, and avant-garde influences, Bent Knee is an American art rock band formed at the Berklee College of Music in 2009.

At the forefront is vocalist Courtney Swain, whose powerful performance on Holy Ghost from their album Land Animal captures the band's experimental spirit.

Stay for the Ending District 97

Formed in 2006 in Chicago, Illinois and featuring 2007 American Idol Top 10 finalist Leslie Hunt, District 97 fuses influences ranging from heavy metal to avant-garde jazz.

Their song Stay for the Ending, from their 2023 album of the same name, encapsulates their blending of styles.

Cause We've Ended As Lovers Jeff Beck

While our list is heavily focused on female vocalists, we must acknowledge the extraordinary contribution of bassist Tal Wilkenfeld on Jeff Beck's Cause We Ended as Lovers, recorded at Ronnie Scott's in London in 2007 when she was just 20 years old.

Tal has performed with Chick Corea, Herbie Hancock, and Wayne Shorter, and appears on Prince's 2021 album Welcome 2 America, the first posthumous release featuring previously unreleased music recorded in 2010.

FEBRUARY 2025

Firefly Solstice

Solstice, founded by composer and guitarist Andy Glass, was voted Best Band in Prog magazine's latest Reader's Poll. Their new album, Clann, is set for release on April 4th, 2025.

The first single, Firefly, perfectly captures their signature blend of high-intensity prog-folk, soaring new age, and fresh, funky pop-rock with world music influences.

Savior Jason Bieler & The Baron Von Bielski Orchestra

Jason Bieler & The Baron Von Bielski Orchestra return with The Escapologist, released on February 21, 2025. The first single, Savior, showcases the band's self-billed 'epic, genre-defying prog rock.'

Joining Jason on The Escapologist are guitarist Andee Blacksugar (Blondie, KMFDM, Peter Murphy), drummer Edu Cominato (Geoff Tate, Mr. Big, Soto), and bassist Johan Niemann (Evergrey).

No Dominion IQ

British progressive rock band IQ, founded in Southampton, England in 1981, returns with Dominion, their 13th studio album, set for release on March 28, 2025.

Their new single, No Dominion, offers a first look at what's to come.

Gods Of Pangaea Tiktaalika (Haken's Charlie Griffiths)

Haken guitarist Charlie Griffiths returns with Gods of Pangaea, the follow-up to his 2022 solo album, this time under the moniker Tiktaalika. Set for release on March 14, 2025, the album features drummer Darby Todd (Devin Townsend) and bassist Conner Green (Haken), along with a powerhouse lineup of vocalists.

The title track, Gods of Pangaea, has been launched, featuring vocals from Texture's Daniël de Jongh. Griffiths describes the album as a more song-oriented approach, balancing thrash, prog, sludge, and softer moments, with every riff driving the music forward.

HOLIDAY 2024

Our meetings are often filled with discussions of bands outside the progressive rock genre, sometimes from the most unexpected places and tastes. That's the message of this year's holiday playlist: an eclectic mix that reflects the Colorado Art Rock Society's love for all kinds of music.

After all, good music is good music, no matter the genre.

December Skies Steven Wilson

Steven Wilson's December Skies is a unique song born from an unexpected collaboration—with AI. Challenged to create a holiday song, Wilson leaned into the experiment, guiding an AI to craft lyrics that feel introspective and evocative, without directly mentioning Christmas.

The result is a piece that captures the loneliness and reflection of the season.

Snowfalls Big Big Train

Big Big Train's Snowfalls is a reflective song written by bassist Greg Spawton in 2017. Spawton describes it as, ...about the loneliness that some people feel at a time of year when others are able to celebrate with family and friends.

The live version, recorded during a soundcheck on their autumn 2023 tour, adds a raw and authentic dimension to this heartfelt composition.

Amazing Grace Jeff Beck

Amazing Grace might not be the first song that comes to mind for the holidays, but in Jeff Beck's hands, it becomes something profoundly moving. His interpretation transforms this classic hymn into a stirring reflection of the hope and introspection that define the season.

Beck's rendition reminds us that holiday music isn't just about carols—it's about the emotions that connect us.

Christmas Time (Don't Let the Bells End) The Darkness

The Darkness's Christmas Time (Don't Let the Bells End) brings a playful spin to the typical holiday song.

With Justin Hawkins' signature falsetto, a school choir's festive backing vocals from Haberdashers' Aske's Hatcham College in New Cross, London—where Justin's mother once attended—and nods to classic Christmas themes, the track blends humor with a touch of parody.

Happy Holiday Enuff Z'Nuff

Enuff Z'Nuff's Happy Holiday adds a psychedelic-flavored power pop twist to the holiday season. Known for their top 40 hits like Fly High Michelle and New Thing, the Chicago-based band brings a nostalgic touch to the holidays.

The song reminds us that the holidays are about more than just presents—it's about love, kindness, and the simple joys of childhood

The Season's Upon Us Dropkick Murphys

Dropkick Murphys' The Season's Upon Us brings a punk rock edge to the holiday season. As bassist Bob Casey states, It's definitely not some cheesy Christmas tune. It's about a dysfunctional family ...and the chaotic family dynamics that many can relate to during the holidays.

NOVEMBER 2024

Landmine Blast Riverside

Riverside have released the first single, Landmine Blast from their upcoming live album Live.ID set for release on January 24, 2025, via InsideOut Music.

As bassist and vocalist Mariusz Duda explains, 'There are a lot of nuances, quirks, and, above all, explanations of what Riverside's music is all about. That's why Live.ID is not just an ordinary live album—it's an album that reveals the band's true identity.'

Opaco Asymmetric Universe

Italy's Asymmetric Universe, founded in 2018 by brothers Federico and Nicolò Vese, aims to push the limits of modern prog by seamlessly blending coherently different genres.

According to the brothers, Opaco, 'is the most technically challenging song we have ever composed. It's inspired by the guitar style of bands like Animals As Leaders and Polyphia, but with genre-bending jazz sections inspired by Pat Metheny, Victor Wooten, and Jacob Collier.'

Broken Kite Parade

Andy Foster, the Somerset-based musician behind Kite Parade has unveiled a video for his heartfelt new single Broken.

Featuring the powerful vocals of Exploring Birdsong's Lynsey Ward, the song embodies the melodic and accessible style that has become Kite Parade's hallmark sound.

§4 Opeth

Opeth's §4, from their latest album The Last Will and Testament, features Jethro Tull's Ian Anderson on flute and spoken word.

Set in the post-World War I era, The Last Will and Testament tells the story of a wealthy patriarch whose shocking secrets are revealed after his death, casting a dark shadow over his family's history.

OCTOBER 2024

All Because Of Me The Pineapple Thief

All Because of Me, by The Pineapple Thief, offers fans a glimpse of the band's upcoming EP, Last To Run set to be released November 8 via Kscope.

'The songs on this EP are based on ideas from the It Leads To This sessions but have since evolved way beyond what we would have imagined at the time. We are delighted with the result—it really seems to capture a different mood and sound' shared vocalist/guitarist Bruce Soord

Blood of the Iconoclast Anima Morte

Anima Morte is a Swedish instrumental band celebrated for their unique fusion of cinematic horror soundtrack elements, inspired by Italian horror films such as Zombie, The Beyond, and Suspiria, with classic progressive rock.

Blood of the Iconoclast, from their 2022 album Serpents in the Fields of Sleep, weaves strong musical motifs to create equal parts atmosphere and groove.

Not Dead Yet Envy of None

Envy of None is a Canadian-American rock supergroup formed in 2021 by former Rush guitarist Alex Lifeson, alongside bassist Andy Curran, vocalist Maiah Wynne, and guitarist/keyboardist Alfio Annibalini.

Their song Not Dead Yet, with lyrics written by Wynne and inspired by Curran's striking chorus line, 'I'm not dead yet, come on along and see what I am good for', is a song that, as Wynne describes, 'embodies resilience and defiance against the forces that aim to tear us down.

Night Terror Dream Theater

Returning with their 16th album—and their first with original drummer Mike Portnoy since 2009's Black Clouds & Silver Linings—progressive metal pioneers Dream Theater need no introduction.

Their new track, Night Terror, is taken from from their upcoming album Parasomnia, which is set to be released February 7, 2025, through Inside Out Music/Sony Music. For better or worse, the track feels like a continuation of the energy and style the band had when Portnoy departed in 2010.

SEPTEMBER 2024

Jainism Devin Townsend

Jainism is the second song to be released from Devin Townsend's forthcoming album, PowerNerd to be released on Oct. 25, 2024.

As Townsend explains, Jainism was based on my connection to music in the mid nineties. Also the idea of what it means to rend yourself artistically to an unresponsive world in 2024. I referenced The Wildhearts in the chorus, as Ginger is one of our generations greatest songwriters.

War of Being TesseracT

TesseractT, hailing from Milton Keynes, UK, are renowned for their complex compositions, dynamic soundscapes, and pioneering role in the djent movement within progressive metal.

Taken from the 2023 album of the same name, War of Being finds the protagonist initially being led to believe in a false reality, only to ultimately reject this deception and begin to question their circumstances.

Preboreal The Ocean

The Ocean is a German progressive metal band known for their blend of post-metal, sludge, and experimental sounds.

Preboreal is taken from their 2023 album Holocene, which, according to guitarist Robin Staps, is an album about the "angst, alienation, loss of reason and critical thinking, rise of conspiracy theories and deconstruction of values in the modern age."

Rogue Jinger

Featuring lyrics that denounce a forced occupation and invasion: “And his order has become their thought,” “Collecting blood is his ambition,” and, “We are forcefully put on his map, War-drum always rattles,” Rogue is a brutal, chaotic, and dissonant track that captures the raw intensity of resistance, crushing riffs, complex rhythms, and Tatiana Shmayluk's fierce vocal delivery to create a powerful anthem of defiance.

AUGUST 2024

Paragraph One Opeth

Opeth have released the first single from their upcoming album The Last Will and Testament, to be released October 11 via Reigning Phoenix Music.

The first single features spoken word by Ian Anderson, and is taken from the album which centers around an unfolding story of a wealthy, conservative patriarch whose last will and testament reveals shocking family secrets.

Life In The Wires Part 1 Frost*

UK progressive rockers Frost* have released Life In The Wires, Part 1

Talking about the album, frontman Jem Godfrey states 'The story revolves around the main character Naio, an aimless kid heading for a meaningless future in an A.I. run world.'

Joining Godfrey on the album is guitarist John Mitchell, bassist Nathan King, and drummer Craig Blundell.

Someone's Daughter Jinjer

Noted for progressive experiments with genres such as metal, R&B, soul, funk, jazz and reggae, and hailing from Donetsk, Ukraine, Jinger have released their new single Someone's Daughter.

Vocalist Tatiana Shmayluk states the song is about 'a world where women are often underestimated and overlooked, they are still powerful heroes who navigate hardship with strength and resilience, unapologetically becoming themselves and breaking barriers in the face of the challenges that face them.'we

Dark and Velvet Nights David Gilmour

Written by David Gilmour and Polly Samson, Dark And Velvet Nights comes from a poem Polly had written. According to Gilmour, 'The music sprang out one day. It was so exciting to have come across this groove. Polly had given me a beautiful poem for our wedding anniversary, which was sitting on the desk right next to me, so I picked it up and sang it on the track, to hear what it would sound like with words. I played it to Polly, and she said, 'Lovely, great, I'll need fiddle with it a bit. So she added a couple of verses, and there it was - very serendipitous.'

JULY 2024

A Boy in Darkness Big Big Train

A Boy in Darkness was recorded at Cadogan Hall during their 2023 UK and European tour.

The live album, A Flair on the Lense, is set to be released on September 13, 2024.

Shine On Jon Anderson & The Band Geeks

Shine is the first single from Jon Anderson & The Band Geeks forthcoming album TRUE, which is scheduled to be released August 23rd, 2024.

Atonement Leprous

Leprous is a Norwegian progressive metal band from Notodden, formed in 2001 by singer and keyboardist Einar Solberg and guitarist Tor Oddmund Suhrke.

Atonement is taken from their upcoming album Melodies of Atonement, set to be released on August 30th, 2024 via Inside Out Music.

Turntail Exploring Birdsong

Hailing from the UK, Exploring Birdsong consists of vocalist/pianist Lynsey Ward, Jonny Knight bass guitar/synth, and Matt Harrison on drums and percussion.

The trio has caught the eyes of the press (Prog Magazine, Kerrang!, Classic Rock Magazine) and achieved two Progressive Music Awards nominations.
